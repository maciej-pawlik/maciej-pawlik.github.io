01 — About

Rethinking resilience
through living systems

I am a part-time doctoral researcher at UCL's Department of Risk and Disaster Reduction, working at the intersection of ecology, urban planning, and disaster science. My research asks a deceptively simple question: what can nature teach us about surviving crisis?

My fieldwork takes me between Glasgow and Kyoto — two cities with radically different relationships to urban nature — exploring how botanical gardens, green corridors, and living infrastructure shape community resilience before, during, and after disaster.

I work with creative research methods, combining ethnographic fieldwork with posthumanist theory, participatory mapping, and policy analysis. I believe disaster science must engage seriously with culture, aesthetics, and the more-than-human world.

Beyond the academy, I am engaged in public education, political advocacy, and practical outreach — including school programmes on emergency preparedness and nature literacy across London.
